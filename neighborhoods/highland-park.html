Highland Park · North Shore

The corridor's most varied village.

Highland Park carries the widest architectural and price range of any North Shore village — mid-century moderns alongside Tudor revivals alongside contemporary lakefront estates, with price bands stretching from entry-level to extraordinary. There is no single Highland Park; there are six. The storied Harris estate at 2441 Woodbridge Lane currently rests here.

Chapter One Highland Park Snapshot

A village of range, breadth, and architectural variety.

Highland Park's median list price of $985K is the corridor's most accessible — but the village median tells only part of the story, since the range from entry-level to estate is the widest on the North Shore. Days-on-market run 30 and sale-to-list ratios sit at 97.9%, reflecting a buyer pool that is more price-sensitive than its southern neighbors. The $385 price-per-square-foot is the lowest on the corridor and a meaningful factor for buyers prioritizing square footage and architectural variety over the New Trier feeder.

Chapter Two Architecture & Schools

Eclectic vocabulary, anchor schools.

Highland Park is the corridor's most architecturally diverse village — significant mid-century modern commissions sit blocks away from Tudor revivals, contemporary lakefront homes, classic American foursquares, and a meaningful inventory of architect-designed ravine houses. The largest price-band range north of Chicago lives within the village boundaries, and the eastern lakefront blocks command premiums that approach the Glencoe price ladder.

Highland Park's high-school students attend Township High School District 113, which includes both Highland Park High School and Deerfield High School. Three feeder K–8 districts serve the village — a structural complexity that nuances the price map, since the strength of the feeder district materially affects sub-market pricing within Highland Park itself.
