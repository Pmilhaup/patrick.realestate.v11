Chapter One Wilmette Snapshot

A village of access, walkability, and velocity.

Wilmette's median list price of $1.18M is the lowest among the New Trier feeder villages, making it the most-cited gateway for families targeting that school district. Days-on-market run just 18 — the fastest on the corridor — and the sale-to-list ratio of 99.4% signals buyers competing into well-priced inventory. Price-per-square-foot of $432 reflects both the smaller average lot and the higher proportion of older, un-renovated housing stock that still trades within the village.

Chapter Two Architecture & Schools

Layered vocabulary, anchor schools.

Wilmette's building stock is the corridor's most stylistically layered — a deep base of vintage brick from the 1920s and 1930s, a meaningful cohort of mid-century homes, and a steadily growing share of modern teardown-rebuilds on the eastern blocks closer to the lake. The village core around Central Avenue is genuinely walkable, with restaurants, shops, and the Metra station all within a short stroll, and the eastern beach blocks command a clear premium.

Wilmette's K–8 students attend Wilmette District 39, which feeds into New Trier Township High School (District 203) — the same anchor district that serves Winnetka, Kenilworth, and Glencoe. The combination of New Trier access at a lower entry price is the single most important driver of Wilmette's velocity.
