Chapter One Winnetka Snapshot

A village of scarcity, walkability, and lake.

Winnetka's median list price of $2.10M reflects both the architectural pedigree of the building stock and the structural scarcity of available inventory. Days-on-market average just 22 — among the fastest on the corridor — and sale-to-list ratios run 99.1%, a signal that well-priced homes transact at or near ask. The price-per-square-foot of $612 is second only to Kenilworth, reflecting the premium attached to walkable, lakefront village character.

Chapter Two Architecture & Schools

Layered vocabulary, anchor schools.

Winnetka's building stock layers Tudor revival, Georgian colonial, and Prairie School across overlapping eras — with significant commissions from the early twentieth century preserved alongside meticulous teardown-rebuilds of the last fifteen years. The lakefront blocks east of Sheridan trade at a meaningful premium to the western half of the village, and the inventory-to-demand ratio is the tightest of any North Shore community.

Winnetka's K–8 students attend one of three feeder districts before entering New Trier Township High School (District 203) — the corridor's anchor district and one of the most-cited reasons families relocate from Chicago's North Side. New Trier's academic record, athletic program, and post-secondary placement are central to Winnetka's enduring price strength.
