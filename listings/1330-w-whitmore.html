The Residence

A house composed
for generations.

1330 W. Whitmore Court sits at the close of a private cul-de-sac in central Lake Forest — stone, slate, and copper resolved into a Tudor-inflected manor that holds its corner of the lakeshore corridor with quiet authority.

The architectural restraint is the point. The lime-washed stone façade reads warm in late light; the copper roofs over the bay windows take on the patina the climate has been slowly painting since the home was built; the slate above the central gable is hand-cut in the European measure. The result is a residence that does not announce itself and, for that reason, will outlast the listings around it.

Inside, the home is composed in three distinct wings — a formal entertaining axis, a private family wing, and a guest-and-staff suite tucked off the porte-cochère. Ceiling heights step from twelve feet in the central hall to ten in the bedrooms, holding the proportion that distinguishes a great Lake Forest house from a merely large one.

A residence the data and the eye agree on:
the kind of house that does not need to argue for itself.

The grounds — just over one and one-fifth acres — were laid by Doreen Atkinson, who has shaped a measured progression of garden rooms around the central paver brick courtyard: a formal boxwood parterre to the south, mature serviceberry to the east, and a private rear lawn framed by mature maples that screen the property from every neighbor. The result is total privacy on what is, by Lake Forest measure, an unusually deep lot.

The Neighborhood

Central Lake Forest.

1330 W. Whitmore Court sits inside the central Lake Forest corridor, fifteen minutes by car from the Metra into Chicago, ten minutes to Lake Bluff and Onwentsia, six minutes from Lake Forest's downtown Market Square, and seven minutes from the Deerpath Inn and the Lake Forest Country Day School campus.

The cul-de-sac itself is one of three semi-private streets that thread off Whitmore — quiet, full of mature oak canopy, and adjacent to the Skokie River Forest Preserve, which provides permanent open space along the entire eastern boundary of the lot.
