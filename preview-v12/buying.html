Buyer-side representation.

A buyer-side practice built on the Sotheby's pre-MLS network, institutional comp analysis, and the conviction that the right home never looks like a deal at the table — it looks extraordinary in retrospect. The math has to come first.

01
Chapter One Discovery

Brief, then baseline.

We open with a long-form discovery brief — village preferences, school constraints, architectural taste, price band, and the deal-breakers nobody writes down. Then a baseline against the live market with the same dashboard I read every morning. By the end of the first conversation you'll have a target-price range that holds up against a five-year baseline — not a Zillow estimate.

STEP · 01
Discovery Brief
A long-form conversation about what you actually want vs. what the spec sheet says you want. Architectural taste, school constraint, lakefront vs. ravine vs. east-of-Sheridan, the price beyond which you don't care.
STEP · 02
Live Market Baseline
The same dashboard I read every morning — median, DOM, SP/OLP, absorption — filtered to your village + price band. You'll see exactly where the market is before you fall in love with anything.
STEP · 03
ZenList Activation
Complimentary Sotheby's pre-MLS access activated for your search. Curated listings arrive in your inbox before they reach public portals — sometimes by 48 hours.
02
Chapter Two Off-Market

The market before the market.

The Sotheby's pre-MLS network and an internal pipeline of "owners considering" surface dozens of storied residences each year that never reach the public market. For the discerning buyer with the right brief, this is frequently where the introduction happens — quietly, on terms the seller would never offer publicly.

03
Chapter Three Negotiation

The number.

Every offer goes out backed by three independent valuation methods — sales comparison, replacement cost, and income-equivalent — that have to converge before I'll endorse the number. From there it's days-on-market analysis on the comparable set, list-to-original-list ratio analysis, and a tailored negotiation built around the specific motivations of the listing side, not the market average. Institutional discipline applied at the table on your behalf.
