Glencoe · North Shore

Ravine houses and the Botanic Garden.

Glencoe is the corridor's lakefront-ravine village — defined as much by its topography as by its architecture. The Chicago Botanic Garden anchors the northern edge, and the village has been the most consistently appreciating sub-market on the North Shore across the past decade. An extraordinary ten-year record.

Chapter One Glencoe Snapshot

A village of ravines, brick, and consistency.

Glencoe's median list price of $1.95M sits squarely between Winnetka's tightness and Lake Forest's range. Days-on-market average 28, sale-to-list ratios run a healthy 98.6%, and price-per-square-foot lands at $548. The defining village-level fact, however, is appreciation consistency — Glencoe has produced the steadiest year-over-year price growth on the corridor across the last decade, with shallower drawdowns and faster recoveries than its neighbors.

Chapter Two Architecture & Schools

Brick vocabulary, anchor schools.

Glencoe's architectural character is shaped by its topography — the ravines that bisect the village from the lake inland have produced a distinct sub-genre of "ravine house," sited on bridged or cantilevered lots above wooded gorges. The dominant building material is the warm brick that gives the village its visual signature, with significant inventory of Tudor revival, mid-century moderns sited into the ravines, and a smaller cohort of contemporary lakefront commissions. The Chicago Botanic Garden on the northern edge is a defining civic asset.

Glencoe's K–8 students attend Glencoe District 35 — a small, well-resourced district that feeds into New Trier Township High School (District 203). The pairing of an intimate K–8 experience with the New Trier anchor is one of the most-cited reasons families select Glencoe over its neighbors.
