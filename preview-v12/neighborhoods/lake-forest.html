Lake Forest · North Shore

Six villages inside one.

Lake Forest is the only North Shore village that holds six distinct sub-markets — the storied East Lake Forest estates, the western parcels beyond Western Avenue, and four neighborhoods between. Each operates on its own logic, its own price ladder, its own architectural lineage. A village of extraordinary range, carried as one corridor.

Chapter One Lake Forest Snapshot

A village of estates, ravines, and lake bluffs.

Lake Forest's median list price sits at $1.32M across the full village — a headline that conceals extraordinary internal range. Estate sales east of Sheridan routinely close north of $5M, while the western sub-markets transact in the $700K–$1.1M band. Days-on-market run a measured 34; a sale-to-list ratio of 98.4% reflects disciplined pricing rather than buyer urgency. The price-per-square-foot of $487 is the corridor's clearest signal of land value and lot scale.

Chapter Two Architecture & Schools

Estate-scale vocabulary, top-tier schools.

The village's architectural DNA was written by Howard Van Doren Shaw, David Adler, and Stanley Anderson — whose commissions across the first three decades of the twentieth century established Lake Forest as one of the country's defining estate communities. East of Sheridan, the lots run to multiple acres, the houses to ten- and twelve-thousand square feet, and the building stock leans Georgian, Tudor, and English country revival. West of Western Avenue, the parcels are tighter, the housing newer, and the architectural register shifts toward classic American suburban and contemporary.

Lake Forest's K–8 students attend Lake Forest District 67, feeding into Lake Forest High School (District 115) — consistently ranked among the strongest public school districts in Illinois. The district is one of the most cited reasons families relocate to the village from Chicago's North Side, and a measurable component of the price premium attached to homes inside the boundary.
